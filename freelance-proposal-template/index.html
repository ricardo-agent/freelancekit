Free freelance proposal template

Most proposals read like essays and lose. This one is built on the 6-part structure that actually wins the work — mirror the problem, sell the outcome, bound the scope, anchor the price. Copy it, fill the brackets, send it.

PROPOSAL — [Project name]
Prepared for [Client name] by [Your name] · [date]

1. THE SITUATION
[Mirror their problem in their words — show you understand it
before you sell anything. e.g. "You're launching in six weeks and
the checkout still drops 30% of carts on mobile."]

2. THE OUTCOME
[Sell the result, not the deliverable. e.g. "Fewer abandoned
carts and more completed orders by launch — not just 'a redesign'."]

3. SCOPE
Included: [the deliverables]
Not included: [what's explicitly out — this kills scope creep]
Timeline: [milestones / delivery date]

4. INVESTMENT
Option A — [lighter option]: [price]
Option B — [recommended]: [price]  ← recommended
Option C — [premium option]: [price]
[Most clients pick the middle — give them a middle to pick.]

5. WHY ME
[One relevant proof point — a result, not your whole portfolio.
e.g. "Rebuilt checkout for a DTC brand last quarter — completion up
22% in the first month."]

6. NEXT STEP
[One clear ask. e.g. "If Option B looks right, reply 'yes' and
I'll hold a start date for next week."]

Why this structure wins

Clients aren't grading your prose — they're scanning for three things: Do they understand my problem? Will this get me the outcome I want? Is the price and scope clear? The six parts answer all three, fast, in the order a decision actually gets made. That's why a plainly-written proposal with this structure beats a beautifully-written one without it. Keep it to one page and lead with their problem, never your bio.
