/redline— input: a 2-page freelance design contract

The skill read the whole contract and surfaced only what matters — the clauses that quietly move risk onto you — each with a plain-English reason and the exact line to push back with:

RISK
§4.2 — "All IP transfers to Client upon execution of this Agreement."
Ownership passes the moment you sign — before you're paid. You lose your only leverage. Push back: "IP and ownership transfer to the Client upon receipt of final payment."
RISK
§7.1 — "Payment due Net-60 from invoice date."
You'd finance their business for two months, interest-free. Push back: "Payment terms are Net-15, with a 1.5%/month late fee on overdue balances."
RISK
§5.3 — "Contractor shall provide revisions until Client is fully satisfied."
No ceiling — "satisfied" is entirely their call, and one project becomes months of unpaid rework. Push back: "Includes 2 rounds of revisions per deliverable; further rounds billed at my hourly rate."
WATCH
§9 — "Client may terminate at any time, for any reason."
Fine — but there's no kill fee, so a cancellation at 80% done pays you nothing. Push back: "On termination, Client pays for all work completed to date plus a [X] cancellation fee."

Everything else in the contract was standard and left untouched. That discrimination — flag the four that matter, ignore the boilerplate — is the skill.
